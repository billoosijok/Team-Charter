Team Rules

  • Member Roles

    Project Manager:

    • Communication with client which includes gather content, purpose of website, making sure both the team and client are clear on timelines, going over initial mock ups with client, then communication client feedback to the team
    • Communication and review with all separate team members on where they are in their tasks
    • Lead team meetings which includes providing agenda 1 day prior, and send out important updates to the team via the Announcement channel in slack (includes recap of weekly tasks)
    • Tracking team member participation
    • Update and maintain overall team project plan
    • Check in with developer, design and UX leads to make sure they have what they need
    • Set meetings with client along the way to review where we are in the project and get feedback.
    • Set up training with client and submit/approve final projects
    • Turn in team assignments in Canvas

    • Helper:

    • assists all leads during the process
    • any data entry needed during the process
    • Extra assistance needed along the way

    Developer:

    Develop template for all pages that comes from the designers. Would be the lead of the development process and then divide out the work as needed to complete the project.

    • leads development process
    • expected to come up with plan that explains steps involved to be able to divide up the work
    • lead developer then as final say on others team members contributions and input
    • Working in Wordpress to take theme mock up and make into working code
    • demo status updates in meeting
    • teaching the team how the development process will work, share ideas on best practices

      • File Organizer/Administrator:

      Define: Manage website files and structure on GitHub. Needs to make sure our files our organized and clean on both GitHub and also our reference Google Drive.

      User Experience and Accessibility Manager

      Works with both the Design and Development team to make sure the site offers a great user experience and is accessible which are both very important issues to the client.

      Designer:

      • General layout
      • Colors
      • Type
      • Communicate features to developer and team to get feedback
      • Communicate mock ups to developer and team
      • Officially own and maintain the style guide
      • Designer will have final say on what is to be presented to the client

  • Purpose Statement

    This team has been formed for several projects including updating the Fire District #6’s website and change platforms. The client would like this new website to be a great resource for the community and not just remain a static site that no one visits. We are also together to complete two skill building exercises that will help prepare us for our project with fire district #6. We hope that these exercises will help us figure out what roles we are comfortable working in, and help us with communicating as a team. Our objective is to use the skill building exercises to create a systematic approach to our final project.

  • Analysis

    Strengths:

    Most of the team members have great foundation in HTML and CSS and a couple members even have more advanced programming and design skills. Those that don’t have the advanced skills in these areas are totally willing to jump in and learn what they need to to assist the team in meeting deadlines. Our team has a lot of opinions and suggestions on how the project should flow which could sometimes be a weakness but is something a team definitely needs; opinions and suggestions. We also have someone with project management experience which will help keep the team organized and accountable

    What needs to be developed?:

    As a team we are planning on developing our experience in real-life projects, which involves learning about group file-management, evenly dividing the workload, and making sure everybody is on track. As a team we plan on taking advantage of tools like git in order to manage our website's files and track the progress of development. We are also using google drive to store our reference materials. Personally, we need to make sure we continue to respect each other’s input and know how to properly respond when something doesn’t go our way. For example, if an idea of mine doesn’t get implemented because the rest of the group strongly feels we should go in another direction, I need to accept that and move on to the next task.

  • Meeting Structure

    Our “in person” meetings outside of class will be held on monday afternoons at 1pm so that we have the opportunity to plan the objectives of the week. The project manager is expected to have an agenda ready by at least the night before. We plan on structuring the meetings with the opening consisting of each member discussing what they have been working on since the last class and discussing any obstacles they’ve experienced. Attendance is required at all meetings. In the case of emergency or bad weather, each member needs to advise the team via Slack so we can either schedule a different time or they can provide their updates to the team lead so they can share during the meeting.

  • Communication

    Communication via slack and shared documents in a shared google drive folder and eventually GitHub. We will also have time in class and our official weekly meeting to make sure we are all in sink.

  • Decision Making Process

    Gather ideas from the group during team meetings, then assessing which path makes more sense for the project's goal while also accounting for our timeline. If we are unable to reach a consensus within the group, the Project Manager/Lead will make the final decision. Besides the Project Manager/Team Lead, there will also be a Developer, Design, and UX lead. They will be responsible for their part, again after hearing feedback from the team. We are tracking our team's progress through a project plan that is in the form of a spreadsheet. We are also creating a planning slack channel where our project manager can send us meeting recaps and notify of of impending deadlines.

  • Expectations

    All team members are expected to participate, share opinions and ideas, and respect each other during the entire process from start to finish. We discussed our schedules/obligations outside of class time and will schedule meetings when all members are available. Non-performing team members will need to speak to the team lead and explain any challenges they are having to prevent the team from falling behind. If a team member is unable to complete a task, we will discuss as a team and redistribute the task if necessary to make sure we stay on track and on time. We are talking about implementing a point system where we all start off with the same amount of points and if we do not complete our task, points will be deducted which then will be noted in the team assessment at the end and during the course of the class.

  • Assessment

    We will be filling out Team Evaluations at the end of this project so each member knows these evaluations are a big part of our grade (300 points). Each member’s effectiveness will be assessed by how well they worked within the group and willingness to put in their fair share of the work. In addition to our team assessment for our final project, our weekly meetings will be an important part in assessing our team's effectiveness. These meetings will be an opportunity for us to discuss what went well and where we need to improve. We have already used our first meeting for responding to the feedback we received on this assignment.
